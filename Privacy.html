Important safety information


Read all the safety information below before using your device
Using unauthorized cables, power adapters, or batteries can cause ﬁre, explosion, or pose other risks.
Use only authorized accessories which are compatible with your device.
Use this device within the temperature range of 0°C ~ 35°C, and store this device and its accessories within the temperature range of -20°C ~ 45°C. Using this device in the environments outside of this temperature range may damage the device.
Do not attempt to modify the plug or power cord on your own, and please disconnect the power supply before cleaning the charger.



Please use a dry, clean soft cloth to wipe the device and its accessories. Do not use harsh chemicals or detergents to clean the device or its accessories.
Do not use external heating equipment such as a microwave or hair dryer to dry the device and its accessories.

Child Safety


Keep the device and all accessories away from children. Do not allow children to play with, chew, or swallow the device or any of its accessories to avoid the risk of choking or suffocation.

Making Emergency Calls

Due to service network variations and other regional differences, the device may not be able to make calls in all areas and under all conditions. Please do not rely solely on the device to make important or emergency calls. Making calls isn't supported on Mi Pad.

Safety precautions


Observe any laws and rules which restrict using mobile phones in speciﬁc cases and environments.
Do not use your phone in petrol stations and explosive atmospheres and potentially explosive environments include fuelling areas, below decks on boats, fuel or chemical transfer or storage facilities, areas where the air contains chemicals or particles, such as grain, dust, or metal powders. Obey all posted signs to turn off wireless devices such as your phone or other radio equipment. Turn off your mobile phone or wireless device when in a blasting area or in areas posted turn off "two-way radios" or "electronic devices" to avoid interfering with blasting operations.
Do not use your phone in the hospital operating rooms, emergency rooms, or intensive care units. Comply with the current rules and regulations of hospitals and health centers. Please consult your doctor and the device manufacturer to determine if operation of your phone may interfere with the operation of your medical device. To avoid potential interference with the pacemaker, keep a minimum separation of 15 cm be maintained between the mobile phone and the pacemaker. To achieve this use the phone on the opposite ear to your pacemaker and do not carry it in a breast pocket. Do not use your phone near hearing aids, cochlear implants, etc. to avoid interference with medical equipment.
Respect aircraft safety regulations and turn off your device on board the aircraft when required.
When driving a vehicle, use your device in accordance with the relevant trafﬁc laws and regulations.
To avoid lightning strike, do not use your device outdoors during thunderstorms.
Do not use your device to make calls while it is charging. Making calls isn't supported on Mi Pad.

Security notice


Update your device’s operating system using the built-in software update feature, or visit our authorized service outlets. Updating software through other means may damage the device or result in data loss, security issues, and other risks.

Reading mode


This feature is available only on compatible Mi Phones.
Reading mode automatically decreases the level of blue light emitted from the screen to make viewing more comfortable for your eyes.
Switching to Reading mode:

There are two ways to turn Reading mode on and off:


1. Swipe down from the top of the Home screen to show the Notification shade toggles, then tap the Reading mode toggle.
2. Go to Settings > Display > Reading mode. On the same screen, you can schedule to turn Reading mode on and off automatically and adjust color temperature.
Useful tips:
1. 20-20-20 rule: It is recommended to take a look at something located 20 feet away for 20 seconds every 20 minutes.
2. Blinking: To relieve eye dryness, try to close your eyes for 2 seconds, then open them and blink rapidly for 5 seconds.
3. Refocusing: It is a good exercise for your eye muscles to look away from your screen to the farthest place that you can see, followed by focusing on the thumb put 30 cm away in front of your eyes for a few seconds.
4. Eye rolling: Roll your eyes clockwise a few times, then take a break and roll them in an anti-clockwise direction.
5. Palming: Rub your palms together to generate heat before gently pressing them against your eyes for a few seconds.
